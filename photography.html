Safety first

If you are using a mobile phone as a camera, be sure that the flight mode is activated during take-off and landing. If you are using a camera with Wi-Fi or Bluetooth capability, make sure that all wireless connections are turned off. Ask the cabin crew for additional information

Mind the wings

Avoid seats near the wing. When making seat reservations keep in mind that there is a wing that obstructs the view during the flight. Choose seats at the front or the back of the plane for larger unobstructed viewing angles.

Be prepared

On planes, sometimes the windows are not what one would call clean. Pictures taken out of a stained window might appear blurry or foggy. A napkin often cannot remove all the stains. Use a glasses wet wipe instead. Those products remove even greasy layers from the window.

Cut the flash

Turn off your flash. This way you won’t ruin your pictures by reflecting your flash on the window. Plus you don't disturb other passangers sitting near you.

It's all about the contrast

Shoot in HDR mode. Eventual hazy skies plus the all the surfaces on the window decrease the contrast in your photos. You can either post adjust your images or use the HDR mode right away. This setting can be found on most smartphones and cameras.

Take control over the focus

Set your camera to manual focus, especially in low light conditions. This prevents your camera to constantly trying to focus on the window surface or its reflections. The latest smartphone cameras support manual focus.

Get under cover

In low-light situations such as sunsets or night city views, cover the window with your jacket or use the provided blanket. Take pictures under the cover that you just set up. With this trick you’ll eliminate all the reflections from inside the cabin.

Appreciate turns

Take advantage of take-offs and landings. These are the moment when you get to enjoy great city views from a relatively low altitude. In addition, when the plane takes turns you might find a moment where you can take a couple of shots facing almost 90 degrees downwards.
